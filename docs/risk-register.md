# Risk Register

| Risk Name                                  | Category     | Likelihood                                                                                                                                | Impact                                                                                                                      | Mitigation                                                                                                                                                                                                                          | Signal of Success                                                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------------ | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Distribution Shift**                     | AI-Technical | **High** – Patient conditions may change over time or differ from the 18 months of Mercer training data.                                  | **High** – The model may incorrectly classify high-risk patients, delaying treatment.                                       | The data science team will evaluate model performance every three months using recent patient data. If recall drops by more than 3 percentage points, deployment will be paused until the model is retrained.                       | Model recall remains within ±3 percentage points of the validation baseline during quarterly reviews.                            |
| **Lack of External Validation**            | AI-Technical | **High** – Internal testing alone may not identify weaknesses before deployment, as demonstrated by the Epic Sepsis Model.                | **High** – An unreliable model could miss critically ill patients and reduce clinician trust.                               | Independent researchers will validate the model before deployment and after every major update. Deployment will only proceed if predefined performance thresholds are achieved.                                                     | Independent validation confirms recall and sensitivity remain within ±3 percentage points of internal testing before deployment. |
| **Alert Fatigue**                          | Operational  | **High** – Excessive false-positive alerts may cause clinicians to ignore future warnings.                                                | **High** – Genuine high-risk patients may experience delayed treatment because important alerts are dismissed.              | Clinical leads will review alert thresholds every month. If fewer than 70% of alerts lead to clinical action, thresholds will be recalibrated before continued use.                                                                 | At least 70% of AI alerts result in an appropriate clinical response each month.                                                 |
| **Insufficient Staff Training**            | Operational  | **Medium** – Staff unfamiliar with the AI system may misuse or ignore recommendations.                                                    | **Medium** – Incorrect use of the model could reduce patient safety and workflow efficiency.                                | All emergency department staff must complete AI competency training before accessing the system, with mandatory annual refresher training.                                                                                          | 100% of clinical staff complete competency training before using the system.                                                     |
| **Automation Bias**                        | Ethical      | **Medium** – Clinicians may rely on AI recommendations instead of applying their own clinical judgement.                                  | **High** – Incorrect AI recommendations may be accepted without recognising obvious clinical warning signs.                 | Emergency department staff will complete simulation exercises every six months demonstrating when AI recommendations should be overridden using clinical judgement.                                                                 | Clinicians correctly override incorrect AI recommendations in at least 90% of simulation exercises.                              |
| **Patient Transparency and Consent**       | Ethical      | **Medium** – Patients may not realise that AI contributes to their triage assessment.                                                     | **Medium** – Lack of transparency may reduce patient trust and confidence in the healthcare service.                        | Mercer Hospital will provide patient information explaining how AI supports, but does not replace, clinician decision-making. Staff will receive training to answer patient questions about the system.                             | Annual patient surveys show at least 90% of patients understand AI assists clinicians but does not make decisions independently. |
| **Under-representation of Patient Groups** | Equity       | **Medium** – Elderly patients, patients with multiple comorbidities, or minority groups may be under-represented in the training dataset. | **High** – These groups may receive less accurate triage predictions, increasing the risk of delayed treatment.             | Model performance will be evaluated separately for each demographic subgroup before deployment. If any subgroup shows a recall gap greater than 5 percentage points, the model will be retrained before deployment.                 | Recall gap between any subgroup and the overall model recall remains below 5 percentage points during quarterly evaluation.      |
| **Proxy Label Bias**                       | Equity       | **Medium** – Historical triage decisions used as training labels may reflect existing clinician bias rather than true patient need.       | **High** – The model may continue to under-triage certain patient groups because it has learned biased historical patterns. | Before deployment, the data science team will audit training labels for demographic bias. If any group has a recall gap greater than 5 percentage points, the dataset will be rebalanced and the model retrained before deployment. | Recall gap between demographic groups remains below 5 percentage points after bias auditing and quarterly evaluation.            |
